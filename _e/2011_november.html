Missions "One Day to Feed The World"

Sunday, November 20th
One Day To Feed The World is our chance to provide life-sustaining food for thousands of people suffering from malnutrition. Previously, Portland Christian Center was able to provide immediate help in Haiti following a devastating earthquake. Join us on Sunday, November 20th as we welcome special guest Bryan McLees, spokesperson for One Day to Feed the World. Then, join us again on November 27th with your special Thanksgiving Offering as we make an impact in the lives of the broken and hungry of our world.

Bedside Buddies

The women of Portland Christian Center are partnering with Hilltop Christian Preschool & Kindergarten & Candlelighters for Children with Cancer to provide stuffed animals to children at Doernbecher & Emanuel Hospitals. Collection boxes will be available from November 14th - December 16th in the church lobby & outside the main school office at Hilltop to accept your donation. We're looking for new, small to medium-sized stuffed animals. Because of the compromised immune system of these children, we are unable to accept used stuffed animals. Women's "Christmas Tour of Homes"

Tuesday, December 13th - 6:00 - 9:30 p.m.
Ladies, we're doing a NEW thing! Grab a friend and come to our Christmas Tour of Homes. We will begin the evening at church with a light appetizer, holiday music, instructions for the evening and...a SWAP!! Bring a holiday decoration that is still in good condition that you are no longer using in your home and swap it for something from someone else's home. Then, maps will be handed out and our fun will continue as we sample different goodies at each home that we visit. As we explore, you'll be able to observe all kinds of creative decorations and get some new craft and gift ideas just in time for those last minute holiday events. Oh, and we'll have lots of great door-prizes, too!

Cost: $10 (after December 4th, price will increase to $15). This event is limited to 175 participants. Sign-ups begin this Sunday, November 6th in the church lobby.
